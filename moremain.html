Practice coding regularly. Solve small problems, participate in coding challenges, and build mini-projects. Explore online platforms like Codecademy, freeCodeCamp, and edX for structured learning.

How to start your first project in programming?

Start with a simple idea. It could be a to-do list app, a calculator, or a personal website. Break down the project into smaller tasks. Plan the features and functionalities. Use version control (e.g., Git) to track changes. Seek help from online communities and forums when you get stuck.

Tip:

Start Small. Choose a simple project that aligns with your interests. Break it down into manageable tasks and build incrementally.

Freelance or working for a company?

Freelancing: Offers flexibility, independence, and diverse projects. Requires self-discipline and business skills. Company Job: Provides stability, mentorship, and teamwork. Exposure to larger projects and industry standards.

Tip:

Explore Both. Try freelancing to gain independence and diverse experiences. Consider a company job for stability and mentorship.
